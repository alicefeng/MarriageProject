The Socioeconomic Dynamics of Marriage

In the growing discourse about income inequality in the United States, much has been made about the state of marriage - that it is becoming increasingly concentrated among the well-off and well-educated, that people are pairing off with their socioeconomic peers such that doctors tend to marry doctors or other well-educated professionals. Just how strong is that effect?

Who Marries Whom?

The plot below shows how common (or uncommon) marriages between spouses of various occupations are. For example, 29% of doctors, dentists and pharmacists marry other doctors, dentists or pharmacists while only 0.13% marry chefs, cooks or food preparation workers. 13% of chefs, cooks or food preparation workers marry others with the same occupation while only 0.07% marry a doctor, dentist or pharmacist.
